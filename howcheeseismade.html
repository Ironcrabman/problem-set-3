How Cheese is Typically M


Ingredients

  • Milk
  • Rennet
  • Calcium Chloride
  • Spices
  • Herbs

Process

  1. Milk is extracted from the cow via milking
  2. The milk is then pasturized to remove bacteria
  3. The pasturized milk is put into a cheese vat
  4. The cheese vat is set to an optimal temerature for bacteria culture to grow
  5. Rennet, an enzyme, is added turning the milk into a gel like substance
  6. After the curd has fermented, it is cut into pieces and heated
    1. Heating the curd allows it to seperate from the whey
  7. The whey is then drained out of the cheese vat and only curd is left
  8. Curd strips are then layered and flipped to continue the fermentation process
    1. This process is known as cheddaring
  9. At this point the process differs depending on what cheese is being made
    1. Cheeses like Mozarella get shaped into loaves and placed into a salt water solution
    2. Cheeses like cheddar are made by mixing the curds with salt
  10. All cheeses are then shaped and aged for varying amounts of time
